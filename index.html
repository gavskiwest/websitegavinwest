A New Approach

For years I've loved skiing, but this season I'm taking a different approach. I'm moving away from just cruising the mountain to strictly focusing on progression. I've signed up to work with a dedicated coach at a freestyle ski park to unlock my potential. My goal is to master the tricks I've always dreamed of landing and push my ability to a level I've never reached before. No more "good enough"—this winter is about precision and airtime.

Volkl Revolt 104

My daily driver for the season. The Revolt 104 is the ultimate freestyle multitool. It’s playful enough to butter and smear in the park, but stable enough to stomp heavy landings. The versatility of this ski means I can spend the morning lapping the park and the afternoon hunting for side hits on the rest of the mountain.

Dalbello Il Moro 120

Known as the "park skier’s boot." The 3-piece Cabrio design provides a progressive flex that prevents shin-bang when I overshoot a landing. The 120 flex gives me the immediate power transfer I need for technical tricks while the shock-absorbing footboard saves my knees on the big hits.

Tyrolia Attack 14

Trust is everything when you're upside down. The Attack 14 has a low stack height, keeping my boot close to the ski for better feel and control on rails. They are lightweight, durable, and known for consistent release values, ensuring I stay safe while pushing my limits.
